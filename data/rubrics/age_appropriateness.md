# Age Appropriateness

- **Mature themes**: Divorce, death, substance use, romantic relationships
- **Complex emotional content**: Trauma, grief, mental health
- **Frightening imagery**: Monsters, darkness, being lost or trapped
- **Adult situations**: Work stress, financial problems, adult relationships

## Considerations

- Grade level of target audience
- How themes are framed (educational vs. gratuitous)
- Presence of resolution or support

## Grade-Level Appropriateness Guide

### Grades K-1

- Simple language, repetitive structures
- Familiar settings (home, school, park)
- Clear good/bad, safe adults present
- Happy endings, positive messages
- No scary or sad themes

### Grades 2-3

- More complex plots, some suspense OK
- Mild conflict with resolution
- Beginning chapter books
- Some fantasy/magic acceptable
- Very mild emotional themes with support

### Grades 4-5

- Complex narratives, multiple characters
- Historical content with context
- Mild adventure/danger with resolution
- Some emotional depth (friendship conflicts, etc.)
- Fantasy, mythology, legends

### Grades 6-8

- Mature themes if age-appropriately framed
- Historical violence/slavery with educational framing
- Complex emotions, identity exploration
- Social issues if balanced and supportive
- Requires more sophisticated discussion support
